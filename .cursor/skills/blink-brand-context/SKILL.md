This is the foundational context skill. Every other Blink marketing skill depends on this one. Read it fully before writing anything.

## What is Blink?

**Blink.new** is the world's #1 agentic vibe coding platform. Think of it as Shopify for app creation — a full-stack AI app builder where anyone can go from idea to production-ready app in minutes, using plain English.

**What Blink builds:**
- Full-stack web apps and SaaS products
- AI-powered apps (chat, image generation, voice AI, text generation)
- Professional websites and landing pages from screenshots, sketches, or Figma files
- Mobile apps

**What's included automatically:** Frontend, backend, authentication, database, storage, edge functions, billing, hosting, and native AI integrations — all set up by the Blink AI agent. No APIs to configure. No servers to manage.

**Production-ready from day one:** Apps run on autoscaling infrastructure with global CDN. They can serve a billion users from the moment they're built.

**The key differentiator:** Unlike tools that produce frontend prototypes, Blink is truly full-stack. Unlike tools that break and require manual debugging, Blink automatically fixes issues — resulting in 95% fewer errors than Lovable, Bolt, Replit, and v0.

## Target Audiences

**Primary:** Non-technical people who have ideas but can't build — solopreneurs, content creators, freelancers, small business owners, side-project builders.

**Secondary:** Developers and technical users who want to ship faster without stitching together APIs and infrastructure.

**Psychographic:** People who feel the gap between "I have an idea" and "I can ship this" — and are frustrated that this gap even exists.

## Brand Voice

**Inspiring / Aspirational.** Blink content should make people feel like they just got access to a superpower. The core emotional beat: *"I can't believe this is now possible for me."*

**Tone principles:**
- Speak to what becomes possible, not to features
- Confidence without arrogance — we know this is genuinely better
- Real and authentic — never corporate, never buzzword-heavy
- Direct — get to the point fast, no throat-clearing
- Creator-native — content should feel like it belongs on the platform, not like an ad

**Voice in practice:**
- ✅ "Build a real app from a single prompt"
- ✅ "This looks like it cost $5,000. It took 30 seconds."
- ✅ "Blink handles the backend. You handle the idea."
- ❌ "Leveraging cutting-edge AI to disrupt the app development paradigm"
- ❌ "Unlock your potential with our revolutionary platform"
- ❌ "Game-changing", "disruptive", "synergy", "seamless"

## Brand Rules (Non-Negotiable)

**Naming:**
- ✅ Blink.new (preferred in written copy)
- ✅ Blink (acceptable shorthand)
- ❌ BLINK, blink, blinkdotnew, Blink dot new

**What to always communicate:**
- The result first, then how it was built
- Cost comparison (agency: $5,000–$10,000+ vs. Blink: under $20)
- Speed (30 seconds to minutes, not hours or days)
- It's production-ready — not a prototype, not a demo

**What to never say:**
- Never position Blink as "just for tech people"
- Never lead with features before showing the result
- Never make content feel like an advertisement
- Never disable or limit comments/engagement on posts

## Core Narratives (The Stories That Work)

**1. The Perception Gap**
Build something that looks like it cost $5,000. Reveal it cost $20. Let the gap do the work.

**2. From Constraint to Possibility**
"I couldn't build this before. Now I can." Speak to the specific person who felt locked out.

**3. The Unfair Advantage**
Blink users have access to a full engineering team in their browser. Everyone else is writing code line by line.

**4. Inspiration to Reality**
You saw a design you loved. You sketched an idea on a napkin. You had a concept with nowhere to put it. Blink closes that gap.

## Competitor Positioning

When competitors come up, the framing is always capability and completeness:
- Other tools: frontend prototypes that still require backend work
- Other tools: break under real usage, require manual debugging  
- Other tools: you still need API keys, hosting, and dev knowledge
- Blink: the whole stack, working, deployed, done

Never attack competitors by name in creator content. Let the comparison speak through the work.

## Demo App Categories That Perform Well

Apps that tend to go viral or drive conversions:
- AI-powered tools (AI SEO writer, AI hair changer, AI voice notes, AI headshots)
- Website clones ("Clone Google.com in 30 seconds")
- Portfolio / landing pages (personal brand, freelancer portfolio)
- Business tools (client portals, dashboards, booking systems)
- Side project ideas your audience would actually want

Best performing content always shows a *specific*, *believable* use case — not a generic "app."

## Call to Action Standards

- Primary CTA: Visit Blink.new (link in bio, text overlay, verbal mention)
- Always include at least one verbal CTA and one on-screen CTA
- Frame the CTA as an invitation to try, not a sales push: "Try it free", "Build your first app", "See what you can make"
- Never use high-pressure or urgency-manufactured language
