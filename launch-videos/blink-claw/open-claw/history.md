# OpenClaw: History & Origin

## Creator
**Peter Steinberger** ([@steipete](https://twitter.com/steipete)) — Austrian developer, founder of PSPDFKit (sold to Insight Partners for ~$100M). Returned to indie development in 2025 to build personal AI tooling.

## Timeline

### November 2025 — Clawdbot
- Peter starts experimenting with AI agent frameworks in his spare time
- First version: **Clawdbot** — a personal AI agent accessible via messaging apps (initially WhatsApp)
- Core idea: instead of opening ChatGPT, just WhatsApp your AI and it actually *does things* on your machine

### Early January 2026 — Moltbot
- Clawdbot gains traction in developer circles
- **Anthropic sends a cease-and-desist** (likely trademark / brand confusion with Claude)
- Project renamed to **Moltbot** ("the lobster has molted" becomes the brand mascot/meme)
- Gains 9,000 GitHub stars on launch day (Jan 25, 2026)

### January 25–30, 2026 — Explosive Launch
- **January 25**: Public launch as Moltbot → 9,000 stars day 1
- **January 29–30**: Peak growth — **34,168 stars in 48 hours** (a GitHub record)
- **January 30**: Hits 106,000 stars after rebranding to **OpenClaw**
- Renamed again due to trademark pressure → **OpenClaw** (the final name)

### February 2026 — The Security Storm
- Goes fully viral: 135,000+ instances exposed on public internet
- Security researchers discover **CVE-2026-25253** (zero-click RCE via WebSocket brute-force)
- BrokenClaw research series documents multiple prompt injection exploits
- **341 malicious skills** found in ClawHub marketplace (20% of all skills)
- Andrej Karpathy initially praises it as "genuinely the most incredible sci-fi takeoff-adjacent thing" — then reverses, calls it "a dumpster fire"
- Gary Marcus and other AI researchers issue public warnings

### March 2026 — Stabilization & 200K Stars
- Security patches shipped; rate limiting, stricter marketplace moderation added
- **200,000 stars** by early March 2026
- **250,829 stars** by March 3, 2026
- Surpasses React's 10-year GitHub star record in just 60 days
- Ecosystem of managed hosting providers emerges (Kilo, EveryClaw, ClawBox, Clawctl)
- ClawCon global conference tour announced

## Why "The Lobster" Brand
The lobster metaphor permeates OpenClaw's identity: lobsters shed their shells to grow (molt → Moltbot). The brand embraces "shedding closed-source SaaS shells to embrace an agentic future." The mascot and community call themselves the "Claw Crew."
